Method

Beginner's Method

Examine the cube's face and locate an edge piece that matches the center color.
For example, if the center is white, find an edge with white.
Position the edge piece by rotating the right face (R) clockwise, then turning
the upper face (U) clockwise, and finally rotating the front face (F) clockwise.
Repeat this process for all four edges to create a cross on the chosen face.

In this step, your goal is to finish the first layer of the Rubik's Cube.
Look for corner pieces that have two colors matching the adjacent centers.
For instance, if you spot a corner with both orange and white, and it should
fit between the orange and white centers, you're on the right track.
To achieve this, you'll use a sequence of moves involving the front face
(F) and the upper face (U). By applying these moves and repeating the process
for all four corners, you'll quickly build the initial layer of your solved cube.

In the third step, you'll tackle the middle layer of the Rubik's Cube.
Your goal is to correctly position the middle layer edge pieces.
To do this, look for an edge piece that has one color matching the
center of an adjacent face. Employing a specific sequence of moves
involving the right face (R) and the upper face (U), you'll smoothly
slide the edge piece into its designated spot. This sequence repeats
for all four middle layer edges, effectively placing them where they belong.

The fourth step marks the approach to completing the Rubik's Cube's final
layer. The initial challenge here is to orient the corners, ensuring that
their colors align with the center faces. This is achieved through a
set sequence of moves, including rotations of the right face (R)
and the upper face (U). Once all corners are correctly oriented,
you're prepared for the final showdown of step 5.

The fifth and final step involves permuting the remaining
final layer edges. Your aim is to rearrange these edges
into their proper positions. This step requires a bit more
complexity and uses a prescribed sequence of moves and
algorithms, including rotations of the right face (R)
and the upper face (U). By following these precise
sequences, you'll ultimately reach the coveted solved state
of the Rubik's Cube, where all colors align on their respective faces.

CFOP Method

The first step is to create a cross on one face, usually the white face.
To do this, identify the edge pieces with the white color and
position them correctly, matching them with their respective
centers. This requires precise rotations and maneuvers, including
the use of the front (F), right (R), and upper (U) faces.
Achieving the cross sets the stage for the subsequent layers.

In the F2L step, the solver pairs corner and edge pieces and
inserts them into their proper positions within the first
two layers simultaneously. This technique optimizes efficiency
and is considered one of the most crucial steps in CFOP.
Solvers aim to complete these layers without having to
return to the first layer, increasing solving speed significantly.

Next comes the OLL step, where the solver focuses on orienting all
the pieces on the last layer. This involves recognizing and
executing algorithms to ensure that all the pieces on the
last layer face the correct direction. These algorithms are
essential for efficient solving and bring the cube closer to its solved state.

The final step, PLL, revolves around permuting the pieces on the
last layer. Solvers use algorithms to move individual
pieces to their intended positions while keeping the
orientation achieved in the previous step (OLL) intact.
This completes the Rubik's Cube, aligning all its colors on
their respective faces, marking the successful conclusion of the CFOP method.
